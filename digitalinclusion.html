Bridging the digital divide, building community

A program of Purpose Society, the Digital Inclusion Project provides low-barrier access to devices, the Internet, and one-on-one digital learning support in New Westminster, BC.

WHO WE ARE

The Digital Inclusion Project is a collaborative partnership between the Lower Mainland Purpose Society, Douglas College, and other local organizations working together to close the digital divide across New Westminster by providing key resources, devices, and individualized support.

OUR SERVICES

The Digital Inclusion Hub is a space where folks can familiarize themselves with technology and learn how to demystify the digital world in a one-to-one setting. Access to community and vital services is dependent on our ability to navigate the digital maze. This makes the Digital Inclusion team’s work critical for marginalized populations.

-Terry, Digital Inclusion Hub Client
